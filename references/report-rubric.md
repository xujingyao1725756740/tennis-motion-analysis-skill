# Coaching report rubric

Write the report in this order.

## 1. Bottom line

Give a two- or three-sentence summary. Name the stroke, view, usable repetitions, highest-impact difference, and the main evidence limitation.

## 2. Evidence quality

State frame rate, resolution, camera compatibility, occlusions, contact confirmation, usable repetitions, reference source, and rights status. Say which comparisons are unsafe.

## 3. Phase table

For each phase, include:

- observed player pattern;
- reference pattern;
- normalized measurement where reliable;
- likely consequence;
- confidence.

Do not interpret small differences as faults by default. Prefer ranges and movement patterns over one ideal angle.

## 4. Training scorecard

When requested, show six or eight evidence-backed axes, confidence, an overall training score, and a radar chart. State that this is a coaching-priority score, not a professional percentile or competitive rating. Follow `scoring-ratings.md`.

## 5. Player-level estimate

When requested, give the broadest defensible band and label its scope. For a single-stroke video, use wording such as `NTRP-like technique estimate: 2.5–3.0, low confidence, forehand only`. Never present it as a formal rating. Explain why UTR or WTN cannot be inferred when match results are absent.

## 6. Priority corrections

Choose at most three. For each, give:

1. What is visible.
2. Why it matters.
3. One external cue.
4. One drill.
5. One measurable retest criterion.

Prefer one change at a time. Distinguish a causal candidate from a compensation.

## 7. What already works

Name at least one stable or effective feature supported by the video.

## 8. Retest plan

Request the same view, similar feed, 60–120 fps, and at least five repetitions. For a stronger level estimate, request serve, return, forehand, backhand, net play, movement, and point-play evidence.

## 9. Sources and limitations

List each reference with creator, URL, camera role, and rights status. State that the analysis is not a medical assessment. If the report will be public, identify any third-party media that must be licensed or replaced.
