# Publishing workflow

## HTML report

Include:

1. Conclusion-first summary.
2. Evidence-quality block.
3. Phase-aligned comparison video or still sequence.
4. Phase table with confidence.
5. Six- or eight-axis radar chart when requested.
6. Clearly separated training score and player-level estimate.
7. At most three corrections, plus strengths and retest plan.
8. Linked sources, creator names, camera roles, and rights status.

Use relative paths only when every linked local asset ships beside the report.

## Douyin, TikTok, or Shorts report

Use the HyperFrames video workflow. Default to 1080×1920, 9:16, 30 fps, 30–60 seconds, caption-first, and no narration unless requested.

Recommended beats:

1. 0–3s: one decisive hook tied to the player's visible movement.
2. Evidence: phase strip and one reliable measurement.
3. Angle-matched comparison: player versus primary reference.
4. Angle reset: secondary rear/side view for sequencing only.
5. Myth correction: remove one tempting but unsupported fix.
6. Radar and rating: training score plus the scoped level estimate.
7. Practice plan: three cues maximum and a same-angle retest request.

Keep the primary message above the platform UI zone. Label every professional reference on screen. Show limitations long enough to read; do not hide them in a one-frame footer.

## Rights and attribution

- Record whether each asset is user-owned, licensed, public domain, or restricted/unknown.
- Do not embed restricted or unknown third-party footage in a publicly distributable render.
- Use authorized clips, original diagrams, pose skeletons, licensed stills, or source links instead.
- Attribution does not replace permission.

## Export QA

- Run the video workflow's lint and layout checks.
- Review actual rendered frames at every scene midpoint and transition, not only the browser preview.
- Verify duration, dimensions, frame rate, codec, pixel format, and audio with `ffprobe`.
- Confirm the radar labels, rating scope, source labels, and disclaimer remain readable on a phone.
- Generate a cover frame with a short hook and no third-party face unless authorized.
